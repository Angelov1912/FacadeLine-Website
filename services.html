What we offer

Cladding

It is applied to commercial buildings and cladding in these instances is a non-structural, external surface for a number purposes. For example:
Cladding can create a controlled internal environment or an external façade, often for aesthetic purposes. It can create a weather tight building envelope and prevent the spread of fire. It is also used to provide privacy and security, allow secure access and increase energy efficiency. Of course cladding also allows for ventilation and penetration of daylight.

Curtain Walling

A curtain wall system is an outer covering ofa building in which the outer walls are non-structural, but merely keep the waterout and the occupants in. As the curtain wall is non-structural it can be made of a lightweight material, reducing construction costs. When glass is used as a curtain wall, a great advantage is that natural light can penetrate deeper within the building. The curtain wall facade does not carry any dead load weight from the building other than its own. The wall transfers horizontal wind loads that are incident upon it to the main building structure trough connections at floors or columns of the building. A curtain wall is designed to resist air and water infiltration, sway induced by wind and seismic activites acting on the building, and its own dead load weight.

Windows & Doors

Timber Windows – Timber frame windows have many benefits and advantages as a wood product like wood has excellent thermal efficiency, more character than uPVC and aluminium framed windows. Timber is an environmentally friendly material and much kinder to the environment than uPVC.
Types of timber windows:

  • Softwood- It is suitable for both contemporary and period-style homes and is popular amongst those wanted timber on a limited budget.
  • Hardwood- It is a slower growing type of wood and therefore has a tighter grain than softwood making them more stable and durable. Oak is the most popular and is often used on traditional-style homes.

UPVC Windows – UPVC, also known as Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride, is a low-maintenance building material used as a substitute for painted wood, mostly for window frames and sills when installing double glazing in new buildings, or to replace older single glazed windows. If you’re looking for energy efficiency, UPVC is the perfect choice, as energy rated UPVC windows are extremely thermally efficient.

Aluminium Windows – Aluminium windows are durable, stylish and can be a very long term investment for your home so it’s no surprise that they’re one of the most popular window frame materials available. Aluminium windows are often seen as large floor to ceiling windows in dream houses, offering up a panoramic view of beautiful scenery.

Shopfronts

Aluminium Shopfronts

Aluminium shopfronts are designed especially to meet the needs of commercial ground floor businesses. The aluminium system with slim site lines can provide unique facade solutions to retail outlets, shopping centres and corporate offices. Aluminium fronts can be painted in any RAL colour, and the flexibility of design allows door configurations to be a swing, pivot, rebated, sliding and sliding/folding. Doors can be both manually and electrically operated. Our shopfronts can be glazed using a variety of glass options to suit your specification.

Frameless Glass Shopfronts

Growing in huge popularity, frameless glass shopfronts allow the customer to maintain a modern and contemporary facade. Frameless glass doors can be installed with rails and/or patch fittings coming in a variety of finishes (satin/polished stainless steel, powder-coated, anodised, brass etc) with decorative door handles to match. The special treatment of toughened safety glass to ensure its strength and reliability means a secure front can be installed without the aluminium profile being present within the main area of the shopfront. The full glass look creates an attractive and comfortable work environment and therefore is perfect for internal partitions. Frameless glass is ideally suited to any business where image and maximum exposure is everything.

Timber Shopfronts

Timber shopfront are used to uphold a shop’s traditional appearance and image. These wooden fronts are ideally suited to areas where planning issues arise due to the location and preserved or listed sites are in a conservation area.

Rooflights

Rooflights (sometimes described as ‘roof lights’ or ‘skylights’) are windows built into the roof of a building. They are effective at allowing natural light deep into the centre of a building, particularly where it is not possible to install windows in perimeter walls or where privacy is needed. Where they are openable, they can also be effective at promoting natural ventilation, as they tend to be at the top of buildings and so can benefit from the stack effect. They may also be used to allow access to roofs or to roof terraces.
